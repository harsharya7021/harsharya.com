About

I started my first company, Ninety-Eight Entertainment, out of college — a full-service marketing and talent management agency. We grew it to ₹15Cr+ in revenue, 65x ROI, a team of 18, and a roster of 12 athletes, 500+ influencers, and 60+ F&B and hospitality clients before I exited to head to ISB.

Along the way I signed cricketer Ishant Sharma as exclusive talent, architected his RuPay × IPL partnership (300M+ impressions), ran award-winning campaigns for Happy Dent and Borges, and built a hyperlocal influencer engine that brands like TrulyMadly and Slay Coffee rode for growth.

After business school I joined Intellewings as Founder's Office — owned commercial GTM in MENA, helped close a $1M ARR upsell with Jio, secured 50% of the venture round, and ran an 8-week reset that lifted productivity 40% and cut cloud spend 30%.

Today I split my time between go-to-market and founder advisory work, the early build-out of ISB's first Sports Management course, and writing about Indian sports, talent and the operating playbook I've collected the hard way.

Off the clock you'll find me on a golf course (12 handicap, longest-drive at the BMW tournament — swing still inconsistent), in a notebook designing something, or two books deep — usually one history and one operator memoir, lately falling for Argentine cinema. Born in Ratlam (population ~270K, on the Mumbai–Delhi line, on no one's map) and still mildly surprised when people don't know it.
